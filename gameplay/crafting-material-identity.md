# Crafting: Material Identity

**Related docs:** [Gameplay Concepts](README.md) · [Docs Home](../README.md) · [Project Goals](../project.md) · [Current State](../game-status.md)

Crafting should feel meaningful. Materials matter, and what you end up with should carry its own story.

## What This Means for You

When you craft, the result isn’t just another recipe unlock. It should reflect more.

- **Source matters:** where a material came from can change the item.
- **Process matters:** how the materials are refined and mixed can raise or change quality.
- **Choice matters:** your decisions show up in the final item’s look and feel.

The idea is that two players can use the same recipe and still end up with items that feel different and unique.

## Why This Direction

Crafting shouldn’t be a boring checklist where everything is interchangeable. If the game tracks source and process, the economy and progression gain real depth:

- Trade routes become strategically meaningful.
- Where a material comes from can affect its market value.
- Crafting feels like active world participation, not just clicking through a menu.
