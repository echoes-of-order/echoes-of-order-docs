# Quest Design (Morrowind-Inspired)

**Status:** Accepted

## Problem / Idea

I wanted a quest system that supports a persistent, consequential world: story-driven quests, long texts where they add value, multiple paths and lasting consequences, and options for both narrative-focused and action-focused players.

## Points Considered

- Morrowind-style strengths: long dialogues, faction and political stakes, meaningful choices, world-changing outcomes.
- Contrast with shallow “kill/collect/talk” quests: the aim was immersion, moral nuance, and consequences that persist.
- Inclusivity: full text for story enthusiasts; short summaries and clear objectives for action-oriented players; future accessibility (e.g. text-to-speech) considered.
- Integration with world systems: factions, reputation, and persistent state; no one-off, disposable content where it can be avoided.
- Balance: depth and pacing; clarity of goals; optionality so that players can engage at different levels.

## Outcome

**Accepted.** Quest design for Echoes of Order will follow this direction: story-driven, consequential, with long texts and short summaries. Implementation will follow; no timeline committed.
