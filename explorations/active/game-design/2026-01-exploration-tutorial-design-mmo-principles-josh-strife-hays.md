# Tutorial Design (MMO Principles)

**Status:** Active

## Problem / Idea

I explored how to teach the game without heavy-handed tutorials: hidden or embedded teaching, focus on competence over completion, and principles drawn from established MMO design (e.g. Josh Strife Hays’ “four principles”) so that onboarding feels natural and respectful of the player’s time.

## Points Considered

- Principles: teach through play; avoid walls of text; reward experimentation; let failure be informative.
- Hidden tutorials: cues in the world and UI rather than mandatory pop-ups or linear tutorial quests.
- Competence vs. completion: players should feel capable, not forced to tick boxes.
- Pacing: when to introduce systems; how to support both new and returning players.
- Alignment with the rest of the game: persistence, consequences, and systemic design.

## Outcome

Under evaluation. Tutorial design is a design goal; concrete implementation (quests, UI, flow) is still to be defined.
